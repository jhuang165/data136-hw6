Part 2 – Puzzle

The file PUZZLE contains SHA256 hashes of tokens of the form:

    SHA256(str(key) + token)

where key is a 9-digit number.

Step 1 – Find the key
I searched the 9-digit key space in parallel and tested common tokens such as
"the", "and", "of", etc. against the puzzle hash set.

A match was found for token "the", giving:

puzzle_key = 747585522


Step 2 – Decode the message
Using the discovered key, I generated candidate tokens from a dictionary
(wordfreq top English words) and tested variants with capitalization and punctuation.

Matching hashes revealed most tokens of the message.


Step 3 – Identify the source text
The partially decoded message matched a quotation from:

E. T. Jaynes – "Probability Theory: The Logic of Science" (Preface)

The correct sentence includes the words:
"Pólya’s", "5", "disbelievers", and "circumstances".


Step 4 – Find the misspelling
All tokens except one were matched exactly.

The final unknown hash corresponded to a misspelled version of
"circumstances".

By generating edit-distance-1 and edit-distance-2 variants and hashing them,
I found the matching token:

cirupmstances

Thus:

puzzle_misspell = "cirupmstances"
